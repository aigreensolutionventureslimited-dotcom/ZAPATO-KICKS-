EXPLORING ALTERNATIVE CAREER OPPORTUNITIES FOR NAUTICAL SCIENCE GRADUATES BEYOND ONBOARD SEAFARING

CHAPTER ONE: INTRODUCTION
1.1 Background of the Study

Nautical Science is a professional maritime discipline focused on navigation, ship handling, seamanship, maritime law, cargo operations, and safety management. Traditionally, graduates are trained to pursue careers as deck officers onboard merchant vessels.

However, factors such as limited sea berths, automation, and economic shifts prevent many graduates from securing onboard employment. Exploring alternative pathways is no longer an option, but a necessity.

1.2 Statement of the Problem

The "sea-time" requirement is a significant bottleneck. Without sea-time, professional growth is halted. This lack of information on alternative roles leads to high underemployment among highly skilled nautical cadets.

1.3 Aim & Objectives
  • Identify challenges preventing sea employment.
  • Examine shore-based maritime opportunities.
  • Analyze transferable skills like situational awareness and risk management.
CHAPTER TWO: LITERATURE REVIEW

The global labor market shows a paradox: while there is a shortage of senior officers, there is an oversupply of junior cadets in developing nations. This chapter analyzes the STCW framework and the shift toward the "Blue Economy."

CHAPTER THREE: ALTERNATIVE PATHWAYS
3.1 Port and Terminal Operations

Roles: Port Operations Officer, Marine Traffic Controller, Terminal Planner.

3.2 Maritime Safety & Compliance

Roles: Safety Officer (ISM), Port State Control Assistant, Marine Surveyor.

3.3 Shipping and Logistics

Opportunities in Chartering, Freight Forwarding, and Supply Chain Management.

CHAPTER FOUR: SKILLS ANALYSIS

Technical competencies like Ship Stability and Meteorology translate into analytical roles in Marine Insurance, while leadership and discipline fit Corporate Risk Management perfectly.

CHAPTER FIVE: RECOMMENDATIONS
  • Maritime institutions must integrate "Business of Shipping" into the curriculum.
  • Government should provide tax incentives for shore-based maritime firms hiring cadets.
  • Cadets should pursue professional certifications in Logistics (CILT) or HSE.
